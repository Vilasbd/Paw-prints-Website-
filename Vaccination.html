Dog Vaccination Guidelines and Standards

Which Shots Do Puppies Need?

Going to the vet over several months for a series of puppy vaccinations—and then for boosters or titers throughout your dog’s life—may seem inconvenient, but the diseases that vaccinations will shield our puppies and dogs from are dangerous, potentially deadly, and, thankfully, mostly preventable.


  • Bordetella Bronchiseptica
    • This highly infectious bacterium causes severe fits of coughing, whooping, vomiting, and, in rare cases, seizures and death. It is the primary cause of kennel cough. There are injectable and nasal spray vaccines available.

  • Canine Distemper
    • A severe and contagious disease caused by a virus that attacks the respiratory, gastrointestinal (GI), and nervous systems of dogs, raccoons, skunks, and other animals.

  • Canine Hepatitis
    • Infectious canine hepatitis is a highly contagious viral infection that affects the liver, kidneys, spleen, lungs, and eyes of the affected dog.

  • Canine Parainfluenza
    • This is one of several viruses that can contribute to kennel cough.

  • Coronavirus
    • The canine coronavirus is not the same virus that causes COVID-19 in people. It usually affects dogs’ gastrointestinal systems.

  • Heartworm
    • When your puppy is around 12-to-16 weeks, talk to your vet about starting a heartworm preventive medication.

  • Kennel Cough
    • Kennel cough results from inflammation of the upper airways.

  • Leptospirosis
    • Leptospirosis can be found worldwide in soil and water.

  • Lyme Disease
    • Lyme disease is an infectious, tick-borne disease.

  • Parvovirus
    • Parvo is a highly contagious virus that affects all dogs, especially unvaccinated dogs and puppies less than four months of age.

  • Rabies
    • Rabies is a viral disease of mammals that invades the central nervous system, causing headache, anxiety, hallucinations, and paralysis.

Puppy Vaccination Schedule

Puppy’s Age Recommended Vaccinations Optional Vaccinations
6 — 8 weeks Distemper Parvovirus, Bordetella
10 — 12 weeks DHPP Influenza, Leptospirosis, Bordetella, Lyme disease as recommended by veterinarian
14 — 16 weeks DHPP, rabies Bordetella, Leptospirosis
12 months DHPP, rabies Bordetella, Leptospirosis, Influenza
Annual DHPP Rabies, Bordetella, Leptospirosis, Influenza

Common Side Effects

After vaccination, some puppies may experience minor side effects, including:

  • Fatigue
  • Loss of appetite
  • Low-grade fever
  • Mild swelling at the injection site

Contact your veterinarian if you notice any severe reactions, such as:

  • Persistent vomiting or diarrhea
  • Severe lethargy
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Swelling of the face or limbs

Cat Vaccination Guidelines and Standards

Which Shots Do Kittens Need?

Kittens require a series of vaccinations to protect them from various diseases. Following this schedule is crucial for their health.

  • Feline Panleukopenia
    • A highly contagious viral disease that can be fatal. It primarily affects kittens.
  • Feline Herpesvirus
    • This virus causes respiratory issues and eye problems in cats.
  • Feline Calicivirus
    • This virus contributes to respiratory infections and oral disease.
  • Rabies
    • A fatal viral disease that affects the central nervous system of mammals.
  • Feline Leukemia Virus (FeLV)
    • A serious disease that affects the immune system of cats.

Kittens Vaccination Schedule

Kitten’s Age Recommended Vaccinations Optional Vaccinations
6 — 8 weeks FVRCP (Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia) FeLV
10 — 12 weeks FVRCP, rabies FeLV
14 — 16 weeks FVRCP, rabies FeLV
Annual FVRCP, rabies FeLV as recommended

Bird Vaccination Guidelines and Standards

Which Vaccinations Do Birds Need?

Birds require specific vaccinations to prevent diseases, particularly if they are kept in groups.

  • Avian Polyomavirus
    • This virus primarily affects young birds and can be fatal.
  • Newcastle Disease
    • A serious viral disease affecting birds, leading to respiratory issues and death.
  • Psittacine Beak and Feather Disease (PBFD)
    • This viral disease affects the feathers and immune system of birds.

Bird Vaccination Schedule

Bird’s Age Recommended Vaccinations
6 weeks Avian Polyomavirus
8 weeks Newcastle Disease
10 weeks PBFD
Annual Booster for all vaccines

Common Side Effects

After vaccination, some birds may experience minor side effects, including:

  • Fatigue
  • Loss of appetite
  • Mild swelling at the injection site

Contact your veterinarian if you notice any severe reactions, such as:

  • Persistent vomiting or diarrhea
  • Severe lethargy
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Swelling of the face or limbs
